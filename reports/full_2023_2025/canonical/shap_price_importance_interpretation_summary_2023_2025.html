SHAP Price Importance Interpretation Summary: 2023-2025

This report consolidates the main SHAP, annual stability, price-regime stability, and the canonical BID raw bid-price diagnostic into dissertation-ready findings. It is an interpretation layer, not a new model.

Headline Findings

  • Overall generation-type SHAP is dominated by OFFER-side CCGT_OFFER, PS_OFFER, BATTERY_OFFER and BID-side WIND_BID, CCGT_BID, BATTERY_BID.
  • The most persistent annual drivers include CCGT_OFFER, WIND_BID, CCGT_BID, PS_OFFER, BATTERY_OFFER, BATTERY_BID.
  • Extreme-price regimes surface OFFER-side scarcity/flexibility drivers such as CCGT_OFFER, BATTERY_OFFER, OTHER_OFFER, PS_OFFER, T_DINO_OFFER, LOAD RESPONSE_OFFER.
  • Most leading BID price drivers are positive raw bids where the SO receives, including CCGT_BID, BATTERY_BID, OTHER_BID, T_GRAI_BID, T_PEMB_BID.
  • A smaller set of important BID drivers are negative raw bids where the SO pays, including WIND_BID, IFA2_BID, T_HOWBO_BID, T_SGRWO_BID, T_MOWEO_BID.

Method Caveats To Use In The Dissertation

  • One-hot SHAP values are model associations: when a label-side dummy is active, predicted system price moves above or below the model baseline. They should not be worded as clean causal unit-offer experiments.
  • Regime SHAP explains variation within low, normal, high, or extreme price regimes; it does not by itself explain why the market entered that regime.
  • BID-side system-price effects and raw bid-price accounting are different quantities. Raw bid prices are used for the accounting interpretation: negative bids mean the SO pays, while positive bids mean the SO receives.

Suggested Dissertation Wording

The pooled SHAP model indicates that system price formation is strongly asymmetric by marginal side: OFFER-side CCGT and storage-related features are associated with higher predicted prices, while leading BID-side features are associated with lower-price system states. Annual stability checks show that these patterns are not solely driven by one calendar year. Price-regime analysis adds a more nuanced scarcity interpretation: low-price periods are dominated by BID-side features, whereas high and extreme regimes shift toward OFFER-side scarcity and flexibility features. Finally, the BID raw bid-price diagnostic shows that price association should be separated from bid accounting: many important BID-side price drivers are positive raw bids where the SO receives, while a smaller set of low-price BID drivers are negative raw bids where the SO pays.
